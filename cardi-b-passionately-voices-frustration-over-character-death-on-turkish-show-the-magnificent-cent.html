Cardi B Passionately Voices Frustration Over Character Death On Turkish Show The Magnificent Cent

 Posted on July 9, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  271 words  |  Aldo Pusey

In the past few days, Cardi B has been a frequently featured name in news media, specifically for the release of her newest music video, "Wap," with Megan Thee Stallion. While some fans have been admiring the video's art or voicing their frustration over the video's guest appearances, Cardi B decided to voice her frustration over something else.

In her case, she's not happy with how one of her favorite shows, "The Magnificent Century," killed off one of its characters.

Related: Game Of Thrones, And Every Other Show We Wish Had A Better Ending

"The Magnificent Century" is a Turkish TV series about one family's reign during the Ottoman Empire. The appeal of the show has been far reaching, considering it has aired in places such as China, Russia, Korea, and the United States.

Among those fans in the United States is Cardi B, who has tweeted about the show many times, in the past, even before this latest, dramatic development.

These tweets from July show that Cardi is not a fan of the character Nurbanu, but definitely fiercely supports Hürrem. Given Cardi's passionate opinions on the show, it was no surprise when she went on Twitter today expressing her anger and sorrow at how how one of the show's characters ended up.

(Kösem is one of the show's characters who was killed off by the show creators, according to Cardi B's other tweets.)

While many fans might not have known about the show before, this big celebrity's clearly passionate interest in it might push more US viewers to look up the series and give it a try.

Hopefully they won't get too attached to Kösem, though.
