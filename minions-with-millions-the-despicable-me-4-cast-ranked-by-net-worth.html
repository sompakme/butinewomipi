The Despicable Me crew is gearing up for another adventure with Despicable Me 4, set to hit theaters in June 2024.

Despicable Me 4 will serve as the sequel to Despicable Me 3,which grossed $1.03 billion globally.

A trailer for Despicable Me 4 dropped on January 28th, teasing new adventures and a potential summer blockbuster:

Since its 2010 debut, the franchise has become a box office juggernaut, gaining its own fandom with its heartwarming stories, lighthearted humor, and, of course, the irresistible, meme-famous "Minions."

The franchise began with Despicable Me, followed by two sequels, Despicable Me 2 (2013) and Despicable Me 3 (2017).

The franchise also includes two spin-off prequels, Minions (2015) and Minions: The Rise Of Gru (2022), short films, a television special, video games, and a theme park attraction.

With over $4.7 billion grossed at the box office worldwide, Despicable Me stands as one of the highest-grossing animated franchises of all time – and as the fifteenth highest-grossing film franchise of all time.

This latest installment promises to be bigger and better than ever, bringing back everyone's favorite reformed villain, Gru (voiced by Steve Carell), his adorable and mischievous Minions, and a whole new cast of characters poised to cause some laughter-filled mayhem.

Despicable Me 4 features the voices of Carell, Kristen Wiig, Will Ferrell, and Sofia Vergara; let's delve into the net worths of the stars who bring these animated characters to life on the big screen.

Starting her career at the tender age of four, Joey King is no stranger to the spotlight.

While commercials for Life Cereal and Eggo might have been her first gigs, she quickly graduated to the big screen, starring in films like Ramona And Beezus and The Conjuring.

8 Pierre Coffin

Pierre Coffin's Net Worth (Estimated) - $10 Million

Pierre Coffin is the mastermind behind the gibberish that tickles our funny bones, and the director who brought Gru and his mischievous Minions to life.

With an estimated net worth of $10 million, Pierre Coffin is not just a voice actor; he's the heart and soul of the Despicable Me franchise.

Coffin is the voice of the Minions, the scene-stealing, language-bending characters who have become pop culture icons.

His ability to breathe life into these yellow goofballs, with their nonsensical vocabulary and infectious laughter, is a unique talent that has not gone unrewarded.

Coffin began behind the scenes as an animation artist on films like We're Back! A Dinosaur's Story. By 2004, Coffin's creative spark had ignited, leading him to create his own animated television series, Pat & Stan.

It was this venture that caught the eye of Illumination Entertainment, and the rest, as they say, is minion-filled history.

Coffin co-directed all four Despicable Me films, each a box office success, grossing a combined $4.7 billion globally.

While specific salary details are scarce, it's estimated he earned around $700,000 for his work on Minions: The Rise of Gru.

7 Miranda Cosgrove

Miranda Cosgrove's Net Worth (Estimated) - $10 Million

Miranda Cosgrove was discovered by talent agents at the age of three.

By the time she was seven, she had appeared in over 100 television commercials for brands like McDonald's and Mello Yello, and modeled for various companies.

Her films have grossed over $2.68 billion globally. Cosgrove's first role was in the pilot of Smallville, in which she voiced the young version of Lana Lang.

Cosgrove rose to prominence with her role in the well-received comedy School Of Rock. The film grossed over $131 million at the box office, marking an auspicious start to Cosgrove's career.

Cosgrove's breakthrough continued with her portrayal of Megan Parker on the sitcom Drake & Josh, and later as Carly Shay in the wildly popular iCarly series. According to the New York Post, Cosgrove made an estimated $180,000 per episode.

Given that the series ran for six seasons and aired 97 of 109 episodes created for Nickelodeon, she likely earned anywhere between $1.7 to 1.9 million by the time iCarly wrapped in 2012, making her the highest-paid child actor of 2012.

Myriad reports estimate that Miranda Cosgrove's net worth is $10 million.

In addition to her television success, Cosgrove voiced the character Margo in the animated film series Despicable Me, a role she will reprise in 2024's Despicable Me 4, contributing to her widespread recognition.

6 Steve Coogan

Steve Coogan's Net Worth (Estimated) - $25 Million

Steve Coogan parlayed his comedic chops and extensive Hollywood career into an estimated $25 million net worth.

Coogan's career began humbly in the 1980s, voicing characters for the satirical puppet show Spitting Image, and lending his voice to commercials.

But his knack for humor soon exploded onto TV screens, where he co-created the iconic Alan Partridge character. This socially inept media personality catapulted Coogan to fame, leading to his own sketch show and even a movie.

He transitioned to the big screen, starring in critically acclaimed hits like The Parole Officer and 24 Hour Party People.

Coogan's comedic versatility shone through from his role as Phileas Fogg in Around the World In 80 Days, to playing opposite Ben Stiller in Tropic Thunder.

His impressive filmography boasts over 50 credits, collectively pulling in $6.6 billion at the box office. In 1999, he co-founded the production company Baby Cow Productions.

The company has produced commercially successful films like Philomena, which grossed $100 million at the box office.

Coogan has been nominated for two Oscars and a Golden Globe, and has won BAFTAs for his work.

Coogan has also invested in real estate; his portfolio is reportedly worth over $14 million.

5 Kristen Wiig

Kristen Wiig's Net Worth (Estimated) - $25 Million

Stepping into the limelight with the innovative and prestigious Los Angeles comedy troupe The Groundlings, Kristen Wiig swiftly transcended into a comedic sensation.

Her seven-season stint on the NBC sketch comedy series Saturday Night Live from 2005 to 2012 earned her nationwide stardom.

Wiig's ability to seamlessly blend humor with nuanced performances became her trademark, evident in supporting roles in comedy hits like Knocked Up and Paul.

However, it was her creative prowess that truly set her apart. Co-writing and starring in the critically acclaimed and commercially successful comedy film Bridesmaids, Wiig showcased her multifaceted talents, and the film earned $306.5 million worldwide.

Beyond the big screen, Wiig lent her distinctive voice to animated franchises, including How To Train Your Dragon and the Despicable Me series.

She is set to reprise her role in Despicable Me 4, and Kristen Wiig's net worth is estimated to stand at around $25 million.

4 Stephen Colbert

Stephen Colbert's Net Worth (Estimated) - $75 Million

Stephen Colbert's comedic journey started with Chicago's Second City, where he honed his craft before hitting the big time.

His stint as a correspondent on The Daily Show was his launchpad, but it was The Colbert Report that skyrocketed him to fame.

The Colbert Report ran for nine successful years; it significantly contributed to Colbert's net worth. His satirical brilliance on Comedy Central's news parody series, The Daily Show, initially gained him widespread recognition.

According to Money.com, at the end of his tenure on Comedy Central, Colbert earned $6 million a year. Transitioning to The Late Show in 2015, he initially maintained an annual salary of $6 million.

He later secured a contract extension with CBS Corp in October 2019 which reportedly boosted his yearly earnings to $15 million.

Despite having only seven films on his resume, he's been part of projects that have grossed over $1.7 billion globally. Colbert is set to appear in an undisclosed role in Despicable Me 4.

Colbert's diversified career includes ventures into the publishing industry, with well-received books such as I Am America (And So Can You!) and America Again: Re-becoming The Greatness We Never Were.

These endeavors, combined with his television success, contributed to Colbert's net worth of an estimated $75 million.

3 Steve Carell

Steve Carell's Net Worth (Estimated) - $80 Million

Steve Carell's big break also came as a result of his work as a correspondent on The Daily Show, sharpening his wit and earning recognition.

Carell gained wider acclaim with The 40-Year-Old Virgin, which he co-wrote. It grossed over $177 million, and his $500,000 paycheck marked his transition to leading man.

He played Michael Scott in the sitcom The Office, where he worked as an occasional producer, writer, and director. Carell's original salary for the show was $50,000 to $75,000 per episode.

TV Guide reported that his salary for his last season on the show leaped to $297,000 per episode, translating to $6.5 million for the 22 episodes in which he appeared.

Steve starred in several comedy films, including Evan Almighty (2004), which earned him a reported $5 million.

Appearing alongside Tina Fey in Date Night, Carell was compensated with a $12.5 million paycheck – as well as $15 million for Crazy, Stupid, Love.

Carell has lent his distinct voice to films like Over The Hedge, Horton Hears A Who!, and the Despicable Me franchise. For his starring role as the felonious Gru in the franchise, Carell was paid just $500,000 for the first installment.

He earns around $15 million to $20 million for his appearances in the Despicable Me sequels, and Carell's net worth is estimated at $80 million.

1 Sofia Vergara

Sofia Vergara's Net Worth (Estimated) - $180 Million

Sofia Vergara stepped into show business in the late 1990s, co-hosting television shows on the Spanish-language network Univision.

After numerous modeling gigs and acting in English-language films, her breakthrough arrived in 2009 with the iconic role of Gloria Delgado-Pritchett on Modern Family.

The sitcom propelled Vergara to international fame and also made her one of the highest-paid actresses in America. Between the first and third seasons, she earned $30,000 per episode.

Vergara was paid $90,000 per episode for the fourth and fifth seasons. By the tenth season, the Emmy Award-winning actress had negotiated a deal to earn $500,000 per episode, which worked out to $10 million per season.
