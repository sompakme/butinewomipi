Early Life and Musical Journey

Tego Calderon, born Tegui Calderon Rosario in Santurce, Puerto Rico in February 1972, exhibited a passion for music from a young age. As a child, he moved to Miami, where he nurtured his musical aspirations and studied percussion. Calderon’s unique musical style blends elements of salsa, dancehall, hip-hop, and plena, showcasing his versatility as an artist.

In 2002, Tego Calderon made a significant breakthrough with the release of his debut album “El Abayarde.” This album marked an important milestone in reggaeton music, as Tego became the first rap artist to perform at the prestigious Puerto Rican National Day of Salsa. The album’s success established him as a trailblazer in the genre, and he continued to push boundaries and innovate with his subsequent releases.

Throughout his career, Tego Calderon has released five studio albums, each showcasing his unique blend of musical influences.
