Highlights

  • François-Henri Pinault, owner of luxury brands and esteemed buildings, takes immense pleasure in pampering his wife Salma Hayek with luxurious trips and gifts.
  • Pinault is not just a successful businessman, but also a philanthropist who established the Kering Foundation to support women who have experienced violence.
  • Pinault is passionate about soccer and even owns the Stade Rennais Football Club, and invested heavily in the technology sector through the Red River West investment fund.

Hailing from a family known for immense wealth, François-Henri Pinault has demonstrated an unwavering dedication to ascend to the helm of a prestigious French luxury conglomerate. Pinault proudly owns renowned fashion brands such as Gucci, Balenciaga, Saint Laurent, and Bottega Veneta, along with prestigious buildings like Christie's auction house and the esteemed French winery, Chateau Latour.

Since Pinault's marriage with Hollywood actress Salma Hayek in 2009, the 61-year-old French billionaire has taken immense pleasure in pampering his wife, with whom he shares a 15-year-old daughter, Valentina Paloma. With his individual colossal net worth of $7 billion, it is evident that his love for Hayek knows no bounds, as he frequently whisks her away to lavish destinations and spoils her with everything luxurious.

8 François-Henri Pinault Married Salma Hayek In Three Extravagant Nuptials

Pinault and Hayek tied the knot on Valentine's Day in 2009, opting for a courthouse ceremony that caught Hayek completely off guard after rejecting his marriage proposal twice. Following their intimate wedding in Paris, the couple chose to renew their vows a few months later in the enchanting city of Venice, where they hosted another celebration. Hayek took to Instagram in 2017 to share details, revealing that their wedding party took place at La Fenice, the oldest opera house in Europe.

In 2018, Pinault surprised Hayek once again by organizing a spa day in the tropical paradise of Bora Bora, which turned out to be another vow renewal ceremony.

7 François-Henri Pinault Took His Wife Salma Hayek To Greece For A Luxurious Vacation

Amidst the challenging times brought on by the pandemic, Pinault and Hayek remained undeterred in their quest for respite and embarked on a captivating journey to Greece. Seeking solace from the chaotic world, the couple indulged in a truly remarkable vacation.

Greece, renowned for its allure as a cherished destination for couples, became their preferred sanctuary, offering a rejuvenating escape. Pinault and Hayek found solace in returning to this captivating country, time and again, to immerse themselves in its refreshing ambiance and unwind from the demands of daily life.

6 François-Henri Pinault Accompanies Salma Hayek At Red Carpet Events Wearing The Designer Brands He Owns

While the dazzling and stylish performer effortlessly captures the limelight, her French partner consistently stands by her side during notable red-carpet appearances and prestigious events where she receives recognition. As the esteemed leader of a renowned fashion conglomerate, Pinault himself is no stranger to making a striking impression on the red carpet as the esteemed actress's esteemed companion.

The power couple frequently catches attention as they grace these occasions, often showcasing their impeccable fashion choices, including ensembles from the iconic brand Gucci. Pinault always accompanies his wife during movie premieres, the MET Gala, and the Cannes Film Festival.

5 François-Henri Pinault Is A Philanthropist Through His Kering Foundation

Pinault's contributions extend far beyond his success in the luxury goods industry. One of his most remarkable achievements is establishing and funding the Kering Foundation, a profound initiative dedicated to supporting women who have experienced violence.

Pinault serves as the chairman of a board of directors that strives to provide survivor-centric services, implement preventive programs targeting young individuals, and mobilize the collective networks of board members to combat violence against women.

In 2009, Pinault initiated the Kering Foundation, focusing on addressing violence against women in Europe and Asia. Vanity Fair reported in 2016 that the foundation had already made a positive impact, benefiting over 140,000 women by that time.

4 François-Henri Pinault Owns A Famous French Soccer Team

Aside from his business endeavors, Pinault also finds joy in devoting his valuable time to various passions. Currently recognized as the fourth-wealthiest sports team owner globally and the wealthiest in Europe by Forbes, Pinault holds the esteemed position of president within Group Artémis. This association grants him ownership of the cherished Stade Rennais Football Club, an iconic team in the French Ligue 1. Even his wife, Salma Hayek, was cheering with him during exhilarating matches in France.

In a conversation with The New York Times, Pinault revealed his enduring passion for soccer, which has persisted from his childhood into adulthood.

3 François-Henri Pinault Donated Millions For The Repair Of Notre Dame Cathedral

During the tragic incident in 2019 when the Notre Dame Cathedral was engulfed in flames, Pinault and his family took a compassionate step forward. They made an announcement, expressing their commitment to donate a remarkable sum of 100 million Euros toward the restoration of this revered symbol of heritage.

The Pinault family's generous contribution joined a collective effort, as numerous wealthy individuals in France also pledged substantial funds to support the vital repairs needed for the beloved landmark.

2 François-Henri Pinault Redesigned A Museum To House Their Family’s Art Collection

In April 2016, François-Henri Pinault's father announced that his cherished modern and contemporary art collection will unveil its permanent future residence in the heart of Paris.

After an extensive renovation project, amounting to approximately €108 million, the Pinault Collection secured a 50-year lease. As part of this agreement, the city of Paris will receive an annual sum of $7 million, along with royalties.

François-Henri Pinault emphasized the profound symbolism of the chosen location, situated near esteemed cultural institutions such as the Pompidou Center, the Louvre, and the Musée d'Orsay. The Pinaults hold a strong desire to present art dynamically and engagingly, sharing its transformative power with as many individuals as possible.

1 François-Henri Pinault Invested Millions In The Technology Sector

In 2017, Pinault took a significant step to enhance Group Artémis' investments in the technology sector by introducing the Red River West investment fund, with a remarkable capital of $100 million. This venture capital fund was established to support French start-ups in their pursuit of conquering the American market. The fundraising campaign received participation from prominent figures such as the Bouygues family and American entrepreneurs, resulting in a successful initial round that generated 100 million Euros.

François-Henri Pinault, serving as the Chairman of the Board of Directors of Artémis and CEO of Kering, expressed his perspective on this venture. He emphasized Artémis' proven ability to identify future leaders and provide them with unwavering support while maintaining an unwavering entrepreneurial spirit. The initiative is rooted in France's remarkable strengths in the digital realm, including highly skilled engineers, a thriving start-up ecosystem, substantial seed funding opportunities, and an esteemed reputation for creativity and innovation.

These selected start-ups have already gained recognition among European customers for their products and services, demonstrating their potential for global success.
